Our Commitment

KJMR is committed to maintaining high standards of publication ethics, integrity, transparency, and academic responsibility. The journal expects authors, reviewers, and editors to follow best practices in scholarly publishing, including originality, fairness in review, confidentiality, and disclosure of conflicts of interest.

Author Responsibilities

Originality and Plagiarism

  • Authors must ensure submissions are original and not published elsewhere.
  • All sources must be properly cited and acknowledged.
  • Plagiarism, duplicate submissions, and redundant publications are not permitted.
  • Manuscripts may undergo similarity screening as part of editorial checks.

Data Integrity

  • Data must be accurate, complete, and presented responsibly.
  • Fabrication, falsification, or misleading representation of data is unacceptable.
  • Supporting data should be available for verification when requested.

Authorship

  • All listed authors must have made meaningful scholarly contributions.
  • Authorship order should reflect contributions and be agreed upon by all authors.
  • Ghost authorship and gift authorship are not permitted.

Editor Responsibilities

  • Editors evaluate manuscripts based on academic merit and scope relevance.
  • Editorial decisions must be fair, unbiased, and timely.
  • Confidentiality of manuscripts and reviewer identities must be maintained.
  • Ethical concerns and complaints are handled systematically and transparently.

Reviewer Responsibilities

  • Provide objective, constructive, and timely reviews.
  • Maintain confidentiality and do not use unpublished information.
  • Declare conflicts of interest and decline reviews if not qualified.
  • Report ethical concerns identified during review.

Conflict of Interest

All parties must disclose conflicts of interest (financial, institutional, personal, or professional) that could influence objectivity. Authors should declare conflicts in the manuscript; reviewers and editors should disclose conflicts during handling.

Handling Ethical Violations

Investigation Process

  1. Initial assessment of the concern or complaint
  2. Collection of evidence and relevant documentation
  3. Consultation with editorial board and subject experts
  4. Fair communication with involved parties
  5. Decision based on evidence and ethical best practices

Depending on the findings, outcomes may include rejection, correction, retraction, or notification to relevant institutions.
