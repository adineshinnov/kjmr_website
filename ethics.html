Publication Ethics

KJMR is committed to maintaining the highest standards of publication ethics and research integrity. The journal expects authors, reviewers, and editors to follow ethical best practices including originality, transparency, fairness, confidentiality, and responsible authorship.

Authors’ Responsibilities

Authors must ensure that submitted work is original, properly cites relevant sources, and accurately reports findings. Data fabrication, falsification, image manipulation, plagiarism, duplicate submissions, and misrepresentation are not acceptable. All conflicts of interest and funding sources must be disclosed.

Reviewers’ Responsibilities

Reviewers must treat manuscripts confidentially, provide objective and constructive feedback, and declare any potential conflicts of interest. Reviewers must not use unpublished content for personal advantage.

Editors’ Responsibilities

Editors ensure impartial editorial decisions, manage peer review fairly, and preserve confidentiality. Editorial decisions are based on manuscript quality, relevance, originality, ethical compliance, and contribution to multidisciplinary research.

Corrections, Retractions, and Complaints

If errors are identified after publication, KJMR may publish corrections or clarifications. In cases of serious ethical violations, the journal may retract an article. Complaints and ethical concerns can be reported to the editorial office for review and resolution.
