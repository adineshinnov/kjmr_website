General Formatting Guidelines

  • Paper Title: Use Times New Roman, font size 14, bold.
  • Author Information: For double-blind review, remove author names and affiliations from the main manuscript file. Provide author details in a separate file.
  • Abstract: Heading in italic; abstract text in Times New Roman, size 11. Keep the abstract concise, informative, and outcome-oriented.
  • Keywords: Provide 4–6 keywords separated by commas.
  • Margins: 2.54 cm (1 inch) on all sides.
  • Font: Times New Roman, size 11 throughout the manuscript.
  • Line Spacing: Single spacing; add one line space between major sections.
  • Section Titles: Major section titles in uppercase and bold. Subsection titles in bold and italic.
  • Figures: Center figures; captions below (e.g., “Figure 1: Caption”). Use high-quality images (minimum 300 dpi).
  • Tables: Center tables; captions above (e.g., “Table 1: Caption”). Ensure tables are editable (not images).
  • Equations: Use the equation editor; number consecutively (e.g., Eq. (1), Eq. (2)).

References and Citations

Use bracketed numeric citations in-text, e.g., [1], [2], [3]. Provide complete reference details with consistent formatting.

Book

Stamp M. Information security: principles and practice. John Wiley & Sons; 2011.

Conference Paper

Maes F, Vandermeulen D, Suetens P. Medical image registration using mutual information. Proceedings of the IEEE. 2003;91(10):1699–1722.

Journal

Liang Z, Jaszczak RJ, Coleman RE. Parameter estimation of finite mixtures using EM algorithm and information criteria. IEEE Transactions on Nuclear Science. 1992;39(4):1126–1133.

Corrections and Retractions

Corrections

Corrections may be issued for errors that significantly affect clarity, interpretation, or scientific integrity. Correction notices will be linked to the original article and will describe what was corrected and why.

Retractions

Retractions may be issued to maintain the scholarly record in cases of serious ethical concerns such as plagiarism, data fabrication, or major methodological errors. Retraction notices will remain permanently linked to the article.

Article Withdrawal

Authors may request withdrawal before final acceptance by writing to the editorial office with a clear justification. Withdrawal requests are processed by the editorial board in a transparent and documented manner.

Post-publication Issues

The journal investigates reported concerns (ethical, factual, or procedural). Actions may include issuing a correction, expression of concern, or retraction depending on evidence and editorial review.
